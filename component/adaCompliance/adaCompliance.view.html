Americans with Disability Act Compliance

As of 2019, Title III of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) is being interpreted to include websites as "places of public accommodation." Meaning that websites with significant inaccessible components can be seen as discriminatory against persons with disabilities. This deems many businesses and government agencies non-compliant and therefore open for lawsuits. The majority of these lawsuits come from California and New York State, as they have their own state wide laws that allow more compensation towards the plaintiff.

The good news is that there are ways to update your current site or build your new website with ADA compliance in mind. Besides making your website easier to access for potential customers with disabilities, protecting you and your business, the added benefit is that the community at large will appreciate your compliance and direct others to your site.
